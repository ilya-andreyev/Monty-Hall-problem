Hello! Welcome to the page dedicated to the Monty Hall problem. This is one of the well-known problems in probability theory based on the American TV game show "Let's Make a Deal," and it is named after the host of the show. Imagine that you are a participant in a game where you need to choose one of three doors. Behind one of the doors is a car, and behind the other two doors are goats. You choose one of the doors, for example, number 1. After this, the host, who knows where the car and the goats are, opens one of the remaining doors, for example, number 3, behind which there is a goat. The host then asks you if you would like to change your choice. Logic tells us that the probability of finding the car behind the doors is the same: 50/50. However, if you make several attempts, it turns out that you will win more often if you change your choice from the initial door.
